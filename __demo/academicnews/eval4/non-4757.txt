Fitch Downgrades Its Outlook For Indonesia Ratings to Stable
 Updated Nov. 30, 2001 11:47 a.m. ET 
 
International ratings agency Fitch said Friday it has downgraded its outlook for Indonesia's sovereign ratings to stable from positive. It cited growing concerns about authorities' intentions to explore interest rescheduling with the Paris Club of creditors. Fitch said it was concerned about the potential burden-sharing implications for private creditors of such a move. Fitch said its sovereign ratings of B- for both long-term foreign currency and long-term local currency remain unchanged.
