Photo tour: Sailing ship line Star Clippers' Royal Clipper
None 
 The 227-passenger Royal Clipper, operated by sailing ship line Star Clippers, is the world's largest full-rigged sailing ship. Gene Sloan, USA TODAY 

 None 
 Modeled on the Preussen, an iconic sailing ship of the early 20th century, Royal Clipper is propelled by 42 sails spread across five masts. Gene Sloan, USA TODAY 

 None 
 Completed in 2000, Royal Clipper is 439 feet long and 54 feet wide with four passenger decks. Gene Sloan, USA TODAY 

 None 
 Featuring the sleek and graceful profile of 19th century clipper ships, Royal Clipper can reach speeds of up to 17 knots on sail power alone. Gene Sloan, USA TODAY 

 None 
 In addition to the working machinery of a sailing ship, Royal Clipper's teak-lined top deck (called the Sun Deck) offers three small pools and rows of lounge chairs for passengers. Gene Sloan, USA TODAY 

 None 
 The forwardmost part of Royal Clipper's top deck is open to the public, offering supreme views of the ocean when the vessel is underway. Gene Sloan, USA TODAY 

 None 
 Among the allures of a voyage on Royal Clipper is the opportunity to climb onto the bowsprit nets at the very front of the vessel while it is underway. Gene Sloan, USA TODAY 

 None 
 Small wings containing navigation equipment extend out from Royal Clipper's top deck next to its wheelhouse. Gene Sloan, USA TODAY 

 None 
 Located between Royal Clipper's second (main) and third (middle) masts is the most forward of its three pools. Gene Sloan, USA TODAY 

 None 
 Royal Clipper's Sun Deck also is home to a small outdoor bar. It's located just behind Royal Clipper's middle mast. Gene Sloan, USA TODAY 

 None 
 The largest of Royal Clipper's three pools is located between the third (middle) and fourth (mizzen) masts of the ship. Notably, it has a glass bottom that serves as the roof of the vessel's three-deck-high interior atrium, making it one of the most unusual pools to be found at sea. Gene Sloan, USA TODAY 

 None 
 Royal Clipper's rear pool is near the back of the ship, behind the rear-most of its five masts. Gene Sloan, USA TODAY 

 None 
 A steering stand with a giant wooden wheel is located at the back of Royal Clipper's top deck just behind its third pool. Gene Sloan, USA TODAY 

 None 
 At the very back of Royal Clipper's Sun Deck are stairs leading down to another small deck area that overlooks the ship's wake. Gene Sloan, USA TODAY 

 None 
 Royal Clipper's top deck is awash with ropes, pulleys and brass fittings used to hoist its 42 sails. Gene Sloan, USA TODAY 

 None 
 Royal Clipper's five masts soar up to 197 feet in the air and are criss-crossed with lines used to hoist and trim the vessel's 42 sails. Gene Sloan, USA TODAY 

 None 
 When unfurled, Royal Clipper's 42 sails have a surface area of nearly 56,000 square feet. Gene Sloan, USA TODAY 

 None 
 Among activities available to passengers when Royal Clipper is at sea is a climb up one of the ship's masts. Passengers must wear safety harnesses and are monitored by ship staff. Gene Sloan, USA TODAY 

 None 
 Passengers can climb to a crow's nest that's part way up one of Royal Clipper's masts. The views are stunning. Gene Sloan, USA TODAY 

 None 
 A setting sun splashes light on one of Royal Clipper's two tender boats, which are suspended along the side of the vessel near its top deck. The boats are heavily used as Royal Clipper almost always anchors when visiting destinations. Gene Sloan, USA TODAY 

 None 
 Located one deck down from the Sun Deck on Royal Clipper is the Tropical Bar, a sheltered but open-air watering hole that serves as one of the vessel's main gathering areas. Gene Sloan, USA TODAY 

 None 
 The Tropical Bar includes a seating area that is one of the few places on Royal Clipper where passengers can smoke. Gene Sloan, USA TODAY 

 None 
 Flensburger, a Pilsner-style beer from the town of Flensburg, in the far north of Germany, is on tap at the Tropical Bar. Gene Sloan, USA TODAY 

 None 
 An exterior walkway extends along the sides of Royal Clipper just behind the Tropical Bar. Gene Sloan, USA TODAY 

 None 
 Just steps away from Royal Clipper's Tropical Bar, on the inside of the ship, is the Piano Bar and Lounge. It's the vessel's only interior bar. Gene Sloan, USA TODAY 

 None 
 Coffee and tea are available throughout the day in a small nook in the Piano Bar and Lounge. Gene Sloan, USA TODAY 

 None 
 At the rear of the Piano Bar and Lounge is a wooden bar area with nearly a dozen permanently fixed bar seats. Gene Sloan, USA TODAY 

 None 
 Fresh fruit is available around the clock in the Piano Bar and Lounge. Gene Sloan, USA TODAY 

 None 
 Among the unusual features of the Piano Bar and Lounge are port holes at its center that offer a view of the underwater portion of the vessel's center pool. Gene Sloan, USA TODAY

 The forward portion of the Piano Bar and Lounge features a comfortable seating area with sofas. Gene Sloan, USA TODAY

 None

 None 
 A small boutique offering Star Clippers logo wear and other items is located in nooks around the purser's desk. Gene Sloan, USA TODAY 

 None 
 Just steps away from the purser's desk is a main stairway area connecting several of Royal Clipper's decks. Gene Sloan, USA TODAY
