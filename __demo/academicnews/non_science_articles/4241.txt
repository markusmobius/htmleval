China to U.S.: It’s your fault we are in the South China Sea

BEIJING — Beijing has a message for the Trump administration: The more ships you send to the contested waters of the South China Sea, the more we will bolster our presence there. The warning, delivered in a People’s Daily commentary published Monday, came days after the USS Hopper sailed within 12 nautical miles of Scarborough Shoal, a reef China seized from Philippine control in 2012. “A U.S. ship wantonly provoking trouble is reckless,” it said. It is not the first time Beijing has tried to blame Washington for maritime tensions — it’s a bit of a recurring theme — but the ship’s operation and the blustery response may signal an ominous shift in U.S.-China ties.
