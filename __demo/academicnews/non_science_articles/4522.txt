This Guy Walks Faster Than You Run
Competitive race walker Richard Luettchau II has heard all the jokes—they usually stop once people see him go
 Richard Luettchau II, a competitive race walker, can walk a mile in about six minutes and 40 seconds. He shares some tips for picking up the pace. Photo: Jeff Bush for The Wall Street Journal 
 July 29, 2017 7:00 a.m. ET 
 
 If Richard Luettchau II wore a Fitbit, it might melt down. As a competitive race walker, he clocks steps at a faster pace than the average adult runs. “The movement looks like a wonky tango, so it’s easy to mock,” he says. “But it’s incredibly difficult.”
