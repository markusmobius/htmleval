Gold Prices: Barrick Gold Hedge Unwinds Help Metal Hit Record
Dec 2, 2009 7:57 am ET 
 
The Journal’s Phred Dvorak reports:
Barrick Gold Corp. accelerated plans to eliminate hedges against declines in gold prices, jumping to capitalize on months of gains for the precious metal and freeing up the company to develop even more.
“We’re optimistic on the gold price,” Barrick Chief Executive Aaron Regent said. The strong price “generates higher cash flow which gives us increased flexibility” to develop mines, he said.
Tuesday’s move by the Toronto-based miner, the world’s biggest gold producer, helped boost the metal’s price briefly to a record intraday high of $1,200 an ounce. Gold settled at a record close of $1,199.10, up 1.5%. Barrick’s move pushed up the company’s shares 7.9% to $46.07.
