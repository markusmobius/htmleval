First look: Inside Silversea Cruises' revamped Silver Cloud
None 
 Call it the new queen of the polar regions. Silversea Cruises' 23-year-old Silver Cloud has just emerged from a massive makeover in dry dock designed to transform it into the most elegant polar exploration vessel at sea. Gene Sloan, USA TODAY 

 None 
 The months-long, $40 million makeover of Silver Cloud included the installment of new windows resistant to polar temperatures. The ship's steel hull also received significant reinforcement and a new ice-class bulb was added to the vessel's front to allow for navigation in icy polar waters. Gene Sloan, USA TODAY 

 None 
 The makeover of Silver Cloud left its large deck-top pool area in place. Such large deck-top pool areas are a relative rarity on expedition ships that sail to the polar regions. Gene Sloan, USA TODAY 

 None 
 Silver Cloud's pool deck has two teak-lined hot tubs that are kept steamy hot around the clock. Gene Sloan, USA TODAY 

 None 
 Few polar expedition ships have an outdoor pool bar, an original feature of Silver Cloud that was left in place during the ship's makeover. Gene Sloan, USA TODAY 

 None 
 Even in Antarctica and the Arctic, the Pool Bar will remain open at least part of the day, weather and waves allowing. Gene Sloan, USA TODAY 

 None 
 Adjacent to the Pool Bar is the Pool Grill, a casual eatery that serves burgers and grilled items during the afternoon. Gene Sloan, USA TODAY 

 None 
 During the evenings, the Pool Grill is transformed into Hot Rocks, a table-served restaurant where passengers cook their own meats and fish on heated slabs of granite. Gene Sloan, USA TODAY

 Among the changes made to Silver Cloud in 2017 to ready it for sailing in polar regions was the addition of seat anchors in its restaurants. The seat anchors will keep chairs from sliding during the rough seas that often can be found in the Drake Passage near Antarctica and in some other polar regions. Gene Sloan, USA TODAY
