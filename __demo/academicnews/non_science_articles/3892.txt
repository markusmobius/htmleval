9 plants for your garden that repel mosquitoes
None 
 1. Marigolds. The flowers' aroma not only repels mosquitoes but also squash bugs and tomato worms. enrouteksm, Getty Images/iStockphoto 

 None 
 2. Lavender. The scent of this herb might take you to Provence, France, where fragrant lavender fields bloom from June to August. It's drought resistant, so it can grow in areas with little rainfall after it's established. Getty Images/Purestock 

 None 
 3. Lemon grass. This herb can grow to 4 feet tall and contains citronella. Tuomas Lehtinen, Getty Images 

 None 
 4. Garlic. Planting garlic not only deters mosquitoes, but if you believe in another type of blood sucker, such as vampires, you'll have your own supply of repellent. lzf, Getty Images/iStockphoto 

 None 
 5. Rosemary. Rosemary's pungent scent drives away flies, mosquitoes, and cabbage moths and does well in hot, dry weather. Muenz, Getty Images/iStockphoto 

 None 
 6. Basil. By growing your own basil, you can keep mosquitoes and flies away from your picnic table -- and have a tasty ingredient for your cooking. photosbyjimn, Getty Images/iStockphoto 

 None 
 7. Catnip. Some cats, like Sneed, absolutely adore catnip; mosquitoes don't. Grow it in pots because it can spread rapidly. Susan Strahosky, Getty Images 

 None 
 8. Petunias. A staple in hanging baskets and your grandmother's garden, petunias come in many shades of pink, red and purple and have a licorice-like scent that repels aphids, tomato hornworms and squash bugs. Mariia17, Getty Images/iStockphoto 

 None 
 9. Mint. This hearty herb can become your go-to for mint juleps on Derby Day, but ants and mice hate it. Because it spreads quickly, you might want to plant it in pots. Geo-grafika, Getty Images/iStockphoto
