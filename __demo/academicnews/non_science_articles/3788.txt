'Hoarding vulnerabilities' – Keeping flaws secret
Get the news
 Tuesday’s announcement by WikiLeaks claims the CIA discovered or acquired security weaknesses in tech products and exploited them into hacking and surveillance tools. The agency kept its discoveries secret, a practice WikiLeaks calls “hoarding vulnerabilities.” 
Note: USA TODAY has not yet been able to confirm the document’s authenticity or evidence of government use of these programs. The CIA did not immediately return a call for comment 
 Examples of devices and programs the agency allegedly targeted: 

Smart TVs

Program: “Weeping Angel” 
Background: Developed in conjunction with MI5, the British FBI equivalent of a domestic counterintelligence and security agency. Weeping Angel is a reference from “Dr. Who,” a popular British TV Sci-fi series. 
What could happen: TV appears to be switched off in “fake off” mode, but records conversations and sends them via Internet to hacker’s server. 

Smartphones

What could happen: From infected phones, via Internet, hackers can gather: 
Phone’s geolocation
Audio and text communication
Camera recordings
Microphone recordings

Vehicle control systems
What could happen: Hacker can remotely take control of vehicle with self-driving capability. 

Computer programs
What could happen: Infected computer systems can: 
Track CDs/DVDs used
Infect transferable USB devices
Hide data in photos or other images
Maintain malware programs
