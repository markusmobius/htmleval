Robert Mugabe Still Holds Sway in His Hometown
Updated Nov. 22, 2017 4:08 p.m. ET 
 
KUTAMA, Zimbabwe—Far from the streets of Harare, where thousands loudly welcomed their incoming president Wednesday, Robert Mugabe’s home village clung to its silence.
The wild celebrations that rocked Zimbabwe’s cities and spontaneous outbursts of anger against Mr. Mugabe, who resigned Tuesday after 37 years in power, haven’t reached Kutama, a small collection of homesteads about an hour’s drive from the capital.
