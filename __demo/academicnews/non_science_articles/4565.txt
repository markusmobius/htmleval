No Flaws in Intel’s Earnings
The chip giant still faces concerns about security flaws in its chips, but strong results overshadow worries 
 By 
 Dan Gallagher 
 Jan. 25, 2018 6:18 p.m. ET 
 
There are still questions about the impact of security flaws in Intel Corp.’s chips, but it is still very hard to get around using those chips.
That was readily apparent in the company’s fourth-quarter results delivered late Thursday. Revenue rose 4% year over year to a record $17.1 billion, with adjusted operating income surging by 21% to $5.9 billion. Both came in well ahead of Wall Street’s forecasts.
