Judges Vote to Uphold Graft Conviction of Ex-Brazilian Leader
The court ruling threatens to end Luiz Inácio Lula da Silva’s bid to run for president in an October election that polls show he would win
 By 
 Jan. 24, 2018 1:48 p.m. ET 
 
SÃO PAULO—A majority of the judges on a Brazilian appeals court voted Wednesday to uphold convictions against former President Luiz Inácio Lula da Silva, complicating the popular politician’s effort to run for president again in October. 
Mr. da Silva, who was convicted in July of money laundering and corruption, has denied any wrongdoing.
