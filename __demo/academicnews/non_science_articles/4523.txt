Titans in Tin Cans
Desron 21 won 118 battle stars during World War II and led the U.S. Navy into Tokyo Bay in 1945. Walter R. Borneman reviews “Tin Can Titans: The Heroic Men and Ships of World War II’s Most Decorated Navy Destroyer Squadron” by John Wukovits.
 March 24, 2017 4:49 p.m. ET 
 
 When the Japanese surrendered at the end of World War II, Adm. Bill Halsey might well have led the way into Tokyo Bay with his flagship, the battleship Missouri. Instead he gave the honor to three proud but battered destroyers less than half its size. Better than anyone, Halsey knew that the thin margin of victory in the dark days of Guadalcanal, when America’s first offensive in the Pacific hung in the balance, was due to the plucky energies of these veterans and a handful of their consorts.
