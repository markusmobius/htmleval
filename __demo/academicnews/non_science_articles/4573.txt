‘Turn Out the Lights’ by Julien Baker: Finding Hope in the Darkness
Julien Baker writes and sings of the tumult of life and love from the perspective of a troubled youth.
 By 
 Washington 
 Julien Baker writes and sings of the tumult of life and love from the perspective of a troubled youth—she just turned 22—and does so with an uncanny balance of thought and emotion. On her second album, “Turn Out the Lights” (Matador), which arrives on Friday, Ms. Baker wraps her evocative tales in the sparest of arrangements, augmenting her guitar and piano with a light application of strings and reeds.
