Rep. Pat Meehan removed from Ethics Committee after report he settled sexual misconduct complaint

The former aide accused Meehan of making unwanted romantic advances toward her, and when she didn't reciprocate, he grew hostile, according to the Times, which cited people familiar with her time in the office. The former aide initiated a complaint with Congress' Office of Compliance, began working from home and ultimately left her job, the Times reported. She later reached a confidential settlement paid from Meehan's congressional office fund. Meehan's spokesman, John Elizandro, issued a statement saying the congressman denies the allegations.
