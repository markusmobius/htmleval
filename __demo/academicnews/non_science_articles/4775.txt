Japan Staffing Firm Recruit Agrees to Acquire Dutch Peer in $1.55 Billion Deal
Updated Dec. 22, 2015 9:33 p.m. ET 
 
TOKYO—Japanese staffing company Recruit Holdings Co. said Tuesday it planned to buy a Dutch peer, USG People NV, for €1.42 billion ($1.55 billion), in the latest example of Japanese companies looking abroad for growth.
The companies said Recruit would make a tender offer of €17.50 a share for USG People, which is listed on the Euronext Amsterdam stock exchange, representing a 31% premium over USG People’s closing price Monday.
